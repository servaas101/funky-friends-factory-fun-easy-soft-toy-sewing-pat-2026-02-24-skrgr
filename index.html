About Funky Friends Factory

Funky Friends Factory began as one maker’s search for whimsical, huggable toys that were as fun to sew as they were to give. What started at a kitchen table, drafting quirky animal shapes on scrap paper, quickly grew into a beloved pattern brand as friends and local sewists began asking for copies of each design. Over the years, the collection has expanded to include dozens of characters—from cheeky monsters to cuddly bears—each pattern refined through real-world testing, workshops, and feedback from thousands of sewists around the globe.

Today, Funky Friends Factory is dedicated to making soft-toy sewing approachable, joyful, and endlessly creative. Our mission is to empower beginners and experienced sewists alike with patterns that are clearly written, thoughtfully illustrated, and playful at heart. We value kindness, clarity, and creativity in everything we do, and we envision a world where more people experience the satisfaction of making something soft, silly, and utterly lovable with their own two hands.

Frequently asked questions

Answers to common questions about Funky Friends Factory patterns, formats, and what to expect when you start sewing your own soft toys.

When you purchase a PDF pattern, you receive an instant digital download that includes full-size pattern pieces, step-by-step written instructions, and detailed photos. You can print the pattern at home on A4/Letter paper or use a copy shop. Paper patterns are printed, professionally packaged versions of the same designs that are shipped to you—ideal if you prefer not to print at home. Both formats include seam allowances, layout diagrams, and clear markings, so you can choose whichever suits your sewing style.

Yes. Many customers make their very first soft toy using Funky Friends Factory patterns. Each pattern is written with clear, friendly language and includes step-by-step photos that show you exactly what your work should look like along the way. We recommend starting with designs labeled “beginner easy,” choosing a stable cotton fabric, and taking your time with each step. You’ll learn fundamental skills like sewing curves, clipping seams, and stuffing evenly, and those skills will carry over to every future project you make.

At minimum, you’ll need a sewing machine, basic sewing tools (scissors, pins or clips, needle, thread), toy stuffing, and your chosen fabrics. Each pattern includes a detailed materials list with recommended fabric types, amounts, and any extras such as safety eyes, felt, buttons, or ribbon. Most toys can be made from quilting cottons or fleece, which are easy to find and forgiving to sew. Wherever possible, we highlight beginner-friendly options and share notes on working safely when making toys for young children.

Many makers do sell finished toys created from Funky Friends Factory patterns at markets, in online shops, and through local stores. As a general guideline, you are welcome to sell handmade items you personally sew in small batches, but you may not mass-produce toys or share, resell, or distribute the patterns themselves. We always appreciate a design credit such as “Made with a Funky Friends Factory pattern” in your product descriptions, and recommend checking any additional terms on the main website if you’re building a larger product line.
